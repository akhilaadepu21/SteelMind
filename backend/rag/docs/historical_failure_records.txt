HISTORICAL MAINTENANCE AND FAILURE RECORDS - TATA STEEL PLANT
Document: HIST-MNT-2023-2024 | Maintenance Engineering Department

=== INCIDENT RECORD 001 ===
Date: 2024-03-15 | Equipment: Pump-12 (Blast Furnace Cooling Loop)
Failure Type: Bearing overheating - critical shutdown
Duration of Downtime: 14 hours
Root Cause Confirmed: Lubrication oil supply line blocked by slag contamination
  - Bearing temperature reached 96°C before shutdown
  - Oil sample showed 45% contamination by iron particles
  - Blocked filter was last replaced 18 months ago (spec: every 6 months)
Repair Actions: Replaced SKF 6310 bearings (both drive end and non-drive end)
  Cleaned oil supply lines, replaced oil filter, flushed lubrication system
  Replaced oil with fresh Mobil DTE 25 Grade
Production Loss: 14 hours of BF cooling downtime, production rate reduced 40%
Financial Impact: Rs 2.8 crore direct loss + Rs 0.6 crore emergency maintenance cost
Lesson Learned: Oil filter replacement interval reduced to 3 months
Preventive Action: Install oil particle counter inline monitor on Pump-12, Pump-08

=== INCIDENT RECORD 002 ===
Date: 2024-01-22 | Equipment: Conveyor-B (Sinter Plant Coal Conveyor)
Failure Type: Belt tear at loading zone
Duration of Downtime: 19 hours
Root Cause Confirmed: Tramp metal piece (structural bolt, 80mm length) from upstream
  - Rip length: 2.3 meters longitudinal tear
  - Belt age at time of failure: 4.2 years (replacement due at 4 years)
  - No tramp metal detector was operational (detector offline for 3 weeks)
Repair Actions: Emergency belt splice repair (cold vulcanizing, 6-hour cure)
  Repaired tramp metal detector and tested
Production Loss: 19 hours sinter plant conveyor, partial shutdown
Financial Impact: Rs 1.9 crore production loss
Lesson Learned: Tramp metal detector must be operational condition - mandatory
Preventive Action: Interlock belt drive with tramp metal detector status

=== INCIDENT RECORD 003 ===
Date: 2023-11-08 | Equipment: Rolling Mill Roll Neck Bearing
Failure Type: Catastrophic bearing failure - unplanned outage
Duration of Downtime: 38 hours (extended due to difficulty removing seized bearing)
Root Cause Confirmed: Water contamination in bearing lubricant
  - Water content in grease: 8.2% (acceptable limit: <0.5%)
  - Bearing vibration had been increasing for 21 days prior to failure
  - Vibration alerts were acknowledged but no action taken (operator fatigue)
Repair Actions: Full roll change (emergency roll set installed)
  Bearing housing inspected, found corrosion pitting
  New bearing ordered (28-day lead time exceeded stock, caused extended downtime)
Production Loss: 38 hours rolling mill shutdown
Financial Impact: Rs 7.2 crore (worst incident of the year)
Lesson Learned: Vibration alerts escalating over multiple days MUST trigger mandatory inspection
Preventive Action: Automated escalation protocol if vibration alert acknowledged >3 times without action

=== INCIDENT RECORD 004 ===
Date: 2023-09-14 | Equipment: Cooling Tower Fan (CT-04)
Failure Type: Fan blade fracture
Duration of Downtime: 8 hours
Root Cause Confirmed: Fatigue crack from unbalanced rotation (one blade had erosion)
  - Vibration had increased from 2.1 to 6.8 mm/s over 45 days
  - Trend not flagged in weekly reports
  - Blade material: FRP, 12 years old (design life 10 years)
Repair Actions: Complete fan assembly replacement (emergency replacement from Rourkela plant)
Production Loss: Cooling capacity reduced, affected downstream operations
Financial Impact: Rs 0.9 crore
Lesson Learned: FRP blade life limit must be enforced; vibration trending reports mandatory

=== INCIDENT RECORD 005 ===
Date: 2024-06-03 | Equipment: Pump-23 (Continuous Caster Secondary Cooling)
Failure Type: Mechanical seal failure with water leakage into product zone
Duration of Downtime: 6 hours
Root Cause Confirmed: Mechanical seal face worn (seal age: 3.1 years, life: 2 years max)
  - Water ingress at 8.5 L/min detected
  - Maintenance schedule override had extended seal replacement interval
Repair Actions: Emergency seal replacement (seal in stock)
Production Loss: 6 hours CC-2 downtime
Lesson Learned: Maintenance interval overrides require engineering sign-off
Preventive Action: Hard lock on seal replacement interval in CMMS
