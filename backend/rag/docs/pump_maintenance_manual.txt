CENTRIFUGAL PUMP MAINTENANCE MANUAL - STEEL PLANT OPERATIONS
Equipment Type: Centrifugal Pump | Model Series: CP-3000 / CP-5000
Document ID: MNL-PUMP-001 | Revision: 4.2

=== SECTION 1: EQUIPMENT OVERVIEW ===
Centrifugal pumps in the steel plant are used for cooling water circulation, slag
flushing, hydraulic systems, and blast furnace cooling. Critical pump stations
include: Pump-12 (BF cooling loop), Pump-08 (rolling mill hydraulics),
Pump-23 (continuous caster secondary cooling), Pump-07 (slag granulation).

Normal Operating Parameters:
- Bearing Temperature: 45–75°C (WARNING: >80°C, CRITICAL: >90°C)
- Vibration Velocity: 0.1–3.5 mm/s RMS (WARNING: >4.5 mm/s, CRITICAL: >7.0 mm/s)
- Discharge Pressure: 95–115 bar (WARNING: <90 or >120 bar)
- Motor Current: 85–110% of rated FLA
- Shaft Speed: ±2% of rated RPM
- Oil Temperature: 40–65°C

=== SECTION 2: FAILURE MODES AND ROOT CAUSES ===

FAILURE MODE: Bearing Overheating
Symptoms: Bearing temperature >80°C, burning smell, increased vibration
Root Causes:
  1. Lubrication failure - oil starvation from blocked supply line or pump failure
  2. Over-lubrication causing churning heat
  3. Bearing misalignment (radial/axial)
  4. Contaminated lubricant (water ingress, particulate from slag)
  5. Bearing end-of-life (>25,000 operating hours)
  6. Cooling water circuit blockage
Immediate Actions: Reduce load by 30%, check oil level and flow, inspect cooling
Long-term: Schedule bearing replacement within 72 hours if RUL < 10 days

FAILURE MODE: Excessive Vibration
Symptoms: Vibration >4.5 mm/s, noise, loose foundation bolts
Root Causes:
  1. Impeller imbalance (cavitation erosion, corrosion, deposits)
  2. Coupling misalignment (angular or parallel)
  3. Worn or damaged bearings
  4. Resonance due to foundation issues
  5. Cavitation (NPSH margin <1.0 m)
  6. Recirculation in low-flow conditions
Immediate Actions: Check coupling alignment, reduce flow if cavitating
Long-term: Dynamic balancing, alignment correction, impeller inspection

FAILURE MODE: Seal Leakage
Symptoms: Visible leakage, pressure drop, increased power consumption
Root Causes:
  1. Mechanical seal face wear (>2 years or 15,000 hours)
  2. Corrosion attack from process fluid chemistry changes
  3. Thermal cycling damage
  4. Shaft deflection exceeding 0.05 mm
Immediate Actions: Monitor leakage rate (<500 ml/hr acceptable short-term)
Long-term: Mechanical seal replacement scheduled

FAILURE MODE: Low Flow / Loss of Prime
Symptoms: Low discharge pressure, motor running light, cavitation noise
Root Causes:
  1. Air entrainment / vapor lock
  2. Suction strainer blocked (>70% clogged)
  3. Check valve failure
  4. Impeller wear (efficiency drop >15%)
  5. Suction pipe blockage

=== SECTION 3: STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE - BEARING INSPECTION ===
SOP-PUMP-003: Bearing Temperature Investigation
Step 1: Confirm alarm via local instrument and control room DCS reading
Step 2: Reduce pump load by 25-30% using flow control valve
Step 3: Inspect oil level sight glass - should be at center mark
Step 4: Manually check oil flow rate at bearing housing drain port
Step 5: Take oil sample for contamination analysis (send to lab within 2 hours)
Step 6: Check cooling water flow to bearing housing heat exchanger
Step 7: Using vibration analyzer, record bearing frequency spectrum
Step 8: If temperature exceeds 92°C - initiate controlled shutdown procedure
Step 9: Allow standby pump to take over load (verify standby auto-start armed)
Step 10: Document findings in maintenance log with timestamp

=== SECTION 4: PREDICTIVE MAINTENANCE INDICATORS ===
Remaining Useful Life (RUL) Estimation:
- Bearing RUL = f(operating hours, temperature deviation, vibration trend slope)
- At temperature 80-85°C: RUL estimate 15-25 days (monitor daily)
- At temperature 85-90°C: RUL estimate 5-15 days (inspect every shift)
- At temperature >90°C: RUL estimate <5 days (immediate attention required)
- Vibration increase >20% in 7 days: indicates accelerating degradation
- High-frequency vibration (>1000 Hz spike): inner race defect, RUL <7 days

=== SECTION 6: MAINTENANCE INTERVALS ===
Daily: Visual inspection, temperature/vibration reading, oil level check
Weekly: Vibration spectrum analysis, oil sample if temp elevated
Monthly: Coupling greasing, motor insulation resistance check
Quarterly: Mechanical seal inspection, impeller clearance measurement
Annually: Full overhaul including bearing replacement if >15,000 hours
