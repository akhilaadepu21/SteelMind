Document: FAR-2024 | Prepared by: Maintenance Engineering | Tata Steel

=== FAILURE ANALYSIS REPORT 001 ===
Date: 2024-08-12 | Equipment: Pump-12 Bearing Failure
Classification: Lubrication-induced thermal failure

Root Cause Analysis (5-WHY Method):
WHY 1: Bearing temperature reached 96°C causing shutdown
WHY 2: Because bearing lubrication oil flow was insufficient
WHY 3: Because the oil supply line had 80% blockage from slag particles
WHY 4: Because the inline oil filter was overloaded and bypassed
WHY 5: Because filter replacement interval was 18 months vs. recommended 6 months

Physical Evidence:
- Oil sample: ISO 4406 cleanliness Class 22/21/18 (acceptable: 17/15/12)
- Filter bypass valve: open (contamination > 3.5 bar differential)
- Bearing inner race: heat discoloration, raceway spalling in 30° arc
- Oil carbon content: 2.8% (acceptable: <0.5%)

Failure Timeline Reconstruction:
- Day -45: Filter differential pressure reached 2.8 bar (no alarm set)
- Day -21: Bearing temperature first exceeded 75°C (WARNING acknowledged, no action)
- Day -7: Vibration increased 35% (daily readings not reviewed)
- Day 0: Temperature reached 96°C → automatic shutdown

Key Learning: Set differential pressure alarm at 2.5 bar. Mandatory action on 3 consecutive temperature warnings.

=== FAILURE ANALYSIS REPORT 002 ===
Date: 2024-05-03 | Equipment: Rolling Mill Backup Roll Bearing
Classification: Water-contaminated lubrication failure

Root Cause Analysis:
Primary: High-pressure roll cooling water ingress through worn shaft seal
Contributing: Grease relief pressure too low allowing water backflow
Contributing: Grease replenishment interval too long (48 hrs vs. recommended 8 hrs)

Evidence:
- Water content in bearing grease: 12.4% (limit: 0.5%)
- Bearing corrosion pitting on races: severe (Grade 4 of 5)
- Seal: carbon face worn 4.2mm (replace at 3.0mm)
- Vibration history: increasing trend for 18 days pre-failure

Timeline: Vibration began rising on Day -18, alert acknowledged 7 times without action
MTTR: 42 hours (extended due to corrosion damage to housing)
Production loss: Rs 8.1 crore

Corrective Action Plan:
1. Mandatory seal replacement at 3mm wear (not 5mm as previously practiced)
2. Grease interval changed to 8 hours (was 48 hours)
3. Water content alarm added to grease monitoring system
4. Vibration alert auto-escalate to supervisor if acknowledged >3 times without action

=== FAILURE ANALYSIS REPORT 003 ===
Date: 2023-12-19 | Equipment: Continuous Caster Cooling Pump (Pump-23)
Classification: Impeller cavitation erosion

Root Cause:
System NPSH available dropped below pump NPSH required during low-inventory operation
Impeller inlet had severe pitting erosion (material loss: ~18% of blade surface)

Symptoms Sequence:
1. Noise: crackling/popping at pump inlet (cavitation signature)
2. Discharge pressure fluctuation ±12 bar (normal: ±2 bar)
3. Motor current: 15% reduction (reduced hydraulic loading)
4. Vibration: broadband increase, no discrete frequency peak
5. Flow rate: 20% below setpoint

Repair: Emergency impeller replacement (spare not in stock — ordered emergency, 4-day delay)
Duration: 108 hours total (104 hours waiting for spare, 4 hours for replacement)
Cost of delay: Rs 4.2 crore avoidable if spare had been stocked

Recommendation: Stock one replacement impeller for Pump-23 at all times (lead time: 21 days)
RUL indicator for impeller: Efficiency drop >15% from baseline = replacement within 30 days

=== FAILURE ANALYSIS REPORT 004 ===
Date: 2024-09-28 | Equipment: Conveyor-A Drive Motor
Classification: Insulation failure due to overheating

Root Cause: Motor cooling fan blocked by coal dust accumulation (80% blocked)
Contributing: Ambient temperature +8°C above normal in that section (roof leak = solar gain)
Contributing: Motor running at 108% FLA for 3 weeks (overloaded)

Evidence:
- Winding temperature at failure: estimated 185°C (class B limit: 130°C)
- Insulation resistance: 0.8 MΩ (minimum acceptable: 10 MΩ before energizing)
- Carbon tracking on winding: phase-to-phase short circuit

Timeline to Failure: 3 weeks of overloading + dust blockage

Repair: Motor rewind — 21 days at external workshop
Alternative: Emergency motor swap (no spare available — 14-day procurement)

Cost: Rs 1.4 crore direct + Rs 3.8 crore production loss during rewind wait

Lessons:
1. Monthly compressed air cleaning of motor cooling fins (mandatory)
2. Insulation resistance check monthly if motor current >105% rated
3. Stock one spare motor frame for each critical conveyor drive (>75kW)

=== PREDICTIVE MAINTENANCE CORRELATION DATA ===
Based on 2023-2024 failure history, the following correlations are validated:

Temperature vs. Bearing Life:
- 65°C baseline: 25,000 hours expected life (nominal)
- 75°C sustained: ~12,500 hours (50% reduction — Arrhenius factor 2x)
- 85°C sustained: ~6,250 hours (75% reduction — Arrhenius factor 4x)
- 90°C+ sustained: <3,125 hours (87.5% reduction — immediate action required)

Vibration vs. Bearing Life:
- 2.0 mm/s baseline: normal wear rate
- 4.5 mm/s: 60% life reduction — inspect within 7 days
- 7.0 mm/s: >90% life reduction — shutdown within 24 hours

Combined High Temperature + High Vibration:
Both parameters in WARNING range simultaneously: reduce estimated RUL by additional 50%
Both parameters in CRITICAL range simultaneously: equipment failure likely within 72 hours

Failure Probability Thresholds Validated by Historical Data:
- RUL < 5 days → 94% of cases failed within 5 days without intervention
- RUL 5-15 days → 72% of cases required maintenance within stated window
- RUL 15-30 days → 45% required maintenance; 55% survived with monitoring
- RUL > 30 days → 12% required unexpected early maintenance
