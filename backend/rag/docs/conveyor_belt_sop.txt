CONVEYOR BELT SYSTEM - MAINTENANCE SOP AND FAILURE ANALYSIS
Equipment: Raw Material Conveyor Belts | Document: SOP-CONV-007 | Rev 3.1
Applicable Systems: Ore conveyor, Coal conveyor, Sinter conveyor, Coke conveyor

=== CONVEYOR BELT FAILURE MODES ===

FAILURE MODE: Belt Misalignment / Tracking Issues
Symptoms: Belt drifting to one side, edge wear, material spillage
Root Causes:
  1. Idler rollers seized or misaligned (most common - 45% of cases)
  2. Uneven loading from chute misalignment
  3. Belt splice failure causing uneven tension distribution
  4. Return side material buildup on idlers causing one-sided tracking
  5. Conveyor frame distortion due to thermal expansion
  6. Pulley lagging worn unevenly
Immediate Action:
  Step 1: Reduce belt speed by 20% using VFD control
  Step 2: Identify which side belt is drifting toward
  Step 3: Adjust tail pulley take-up by 5mm increments toward drift side
  Step 4: Check 5 idlers on each side of drift point for seized rollers
  Step 5: If drift persists >30 minutes, initiate controlled stop
Normal Operating Parameters: Belt tension 15-25 kN/m, Tracking deviation <50mm

FAILURE MODE: Belt Tear / Rip
Symptoms: Longitudinal rip, material falling through, emergency stop triggered
Root Causes:
  1. Tramp metal penetration (iron scrap from raw material)
  2. Belt joint failure (vulcanized splice age >3 years)
  3. Impact at loading zone (drop height >1.5m without impact idlers)
  4. Overloading (>110% of rated capacity)
Immediate Action: Emergency stop. Isolate and LOTO. Assess tear length.
Repair time estimate: <500mm tear = 4 hrs (cold repair); >500mm = belt splice replacement 8-24 hrs

FAILURE MODE: Drive Pulley Slip
Symptoms: Belt speed lower than motor speed, burning smell, reduced throughput
Root Causes:
  1. Pulley lagging worn (rubber lagging <8mm remaining)
  2. Belt tension too low (take-up slack)
  3. Material buildup on pulley face
  4. Belt wet from rain/condensation reducing friction
Immediate Action: Check pulley lagging thickness. Increase tension by adjusting take-up.

FAILURE MODE: Idler Roller Failure
Symptoms: Noise (squealing/grinding), local belt damage, vibration
Root Causes:
  1. Bearing failure in roller (seized bearing = fire risk from friction)
  2. Corrosion from water ingress in roller shell
  3. Material packing inside roller
Immediate Action: Replace seized idler within 1 shift - fire risk from heat generation
Replace rate guidance: Replace when noise level >85 dB at 1m distance

=== CONVEYOR MAINTENANCE SCHEDULE ===
Every Shift: Visual walkthrough, check for belt tracking, material spillage
Daily: Check drive motor current, belt tension reading, emergency stop test
Weekly: Idler roller inspection (replace seized/noisy rollers), belt splice inspection
Monthly: Pulley lagging thickness measurement, alignment check with laser
Quarterly: Full belt splice inspection, take-up gravity system check
Annual: Complete idler replacement on return strand, belt thickness measurement

=== PREDICTIVE INDICATORS ===
- Motor current >105% rated AND belt speed <95% nominal: slip imminent, inspect lagging
- Idler roller surface temp >65°C (IR gun check): bearing failure within 48 hours
- Belt splice age >36 months: elevated risk, schedule replacement at next shutdown
- Take-up travel >80% of maximum stroke: belt stretch critical, replacement within 30 days
