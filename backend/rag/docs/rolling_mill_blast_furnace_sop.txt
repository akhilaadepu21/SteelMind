ROLLING MILL AND BLAST FURNACE - CRITICAL EQUIPMENT SOP
Document: SOP-CRIT-002 | Revision 5.0 | Tata Steel Maintenance Engineering

=== ROLLING MILL CRITICAL SYSTEMS ===

ROLL NECK BEARING MAINTENANCE
Operating Parameters:
  - Vibration (axial): 0.5–3.0 mm/s (ALARM: >4.5 mm/s, TRIP: >8.0 mm/s)
  - Bearing temperature: 35–65°C (ALARM: >75°C, TRIP: >85°C)
  - Lubricant water content: <0.3% (max 0.5%)
  - Grease pressure: 2.5–4.0 bar

Failure Mode: Roll Neck Bearing Failure
Root Causes (in order of frequency):
  1. Water contamination in lubricant (50% of cases) - from roll cooling water
  2. Overloading from mill cobble or abnormal rolling force (25%)
  3. Lubrication starvation from blocked grease line (15%)
  4. Fatigue from extended high-speed operation (10%)

Inspection SOP (SOP-RM-011):
  Step 1: Every 4 hours - check vibration analyzer reading on HMI
  Step 2: If vibration increases >0.5 mm/s in single shift - trigger inspection
  Step 3: Draw grease sample from bearing housing (syringe method)
  Step 4: Test water content with portable moisture analyzer
  Step 5: Check grease line pressure at lubricator unit
  Step 6: If water content >0.5% - flush bearing with fresh grease immediately
  Step 7: Schedule roll change at next available window if vibration trend upward
  Step 8: Document in rolling mill log with spectrogram attachment

Early Warning Signs (7-14 days before failure):
  - Vibration increasing >10% per week
  - High-frequency noise (metallic clicking at >500 Hz)
  - Temperature asymmetry between drive and operator side bearings >5°C
  - Grease color change to dark brown/black (oxidation) or milky (water)

=== HYDRAULIC SYSTEM - ROLLING MILL ===
Critical Parameters:
  - Hydraulic pressure: 280-320 bar
  - Oil temperature: 40-60°C (ALARM: >70°C)
  - Oil cleanliness: ISO 4406 Class 17/15/12 or better
  - Filter differential pressure: <2.5 bar (replace at >3 bar)

Failure Mode: Hydraulic System Contamination
Root Cause: Particulate ingress from cylinder rod seal wear, reservoir breather failure
Symptoms: Erratic cylinder movement, valve hunting, excessive filter replacement
Action: Flush system with clean oil, replace all filters, inspect cylinder rod seals
Risk: Mill dimension control failure can cause product quality defects

=== BLAST FURNACE CRITICAL EQUIPMENT ===

STOVE HOT BLAST VALVE MAINTENANCE
Function: Controls hot blast flow between stoves and blast furnace tuyeres
Temperature: Valve operating at 1000-1150°C (CRITICAL equipment)
Failure Consequences: BF process disruption, safety hazard, production loss >24 hrs

Failure Mode: Valve Seat Leakage
Symptoms: Abnormal hot blast temperature distribution, valve skin temperature >200°C
Root Causes:
  1. Thermal cycling fatigue in seat ring (after >50,000 open/close cycles)
  2. Dust buildup on seating surface preventing proper closure
  3. Actuator force insufficient (spring pre-load degraded)
Maintenance Action:
  - Every 6 months: Seat leak test using nitrogen pressure method
  - Every 12 months: Actuator spring tension verification
  - At >50,000 cycles: Mandatory seat ring replacement

BF COOLING STAVE SYSTEM
Failure Mode: Cooling Stave Burnout
Symptoms: Stave outlet temperature >80°C, reduced cooling water flow <2.5 m3/hr per stave
Root Causes:
  1. Internal erosion from burden material abrasion
  2. Thermal shock from freeze-thaw cycling
  3. Copper stave corrosion (pH <6.5 in cooling water)
Immediate Action: Isolate stave circuit, increase parallel circuit flow, notify BF manager
Production Impact: Single stave burnout = monitor closely; >3 staves in zone = consider tapping rate reduction

=== EMERGENCY SHUTDOWN PROCEDURES ===
Equipment Criticality Classification:
TIER 1 (Plant-wide shutdown risk): BF tuyere cooling pumps, stove hot blast system
TIER 2 (Area shutdown): Rolling mill hydraulics, continuous caster cooling
TIER 3 (Manageable degradation): Individual pump stations, conveyor segments

Tier 1 Equipment Failure Protocol:
1. Immediately notify Shift Manager and Plant Manager
2. Activate standby system if available
3. Assess time to safe shutdown vs. continued operation
4. If continued operation: Post continuous observer, 15-minute reading cycle
5. Prepare emergency maintenance team (callout if required)
6. Document all actions with timestamps
